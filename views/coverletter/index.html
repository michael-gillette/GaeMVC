Hi, I build experiences.

I develop, both front-end and back-end code, with the outlook of programming as an art. Elegant code leads to elegant solutions. I began my studies of User Experience at Auctiva. Learning the ins and outs of a language, its best-practices, allows a product to provide top of the line service to customers. I believe the front-end of an app should bear the soul of a product, and communicate effectively with or without so many words what the product or feature does. I know that simple is better than feature creep. Steve Krug's book title says it all "Don't Make Me Think". I say that goes hand in hand with KISS; Keep It Simple Stupid. Two philosophies I take very much to heart in all my code.

I love to learn

When I started in web programming around 12 years old, I began with the idea of building a forum. Some initial research prompted me to pick up PHP and MySQL. From a simple idea I took off. Its a strong language with untold numbers of supporters, quick and simple to utilize with some powerful features. My first 2 contracts and the first in-office position I held advanced my knowledge of PHP, and my ability to look inward and correct my code for production use. Once I began at Auctiva, I had to transition from a interpreted language mindset to compiled language one.

I started off on small pieces of ASP.NET C# (MVC too) in conjunction with my usual front-end duties. Eventually I became quite fluent, occasionally dipping my toe in the TSQL pool. Not often of course, the DBA lifeguards were very helpful whenever I required a certain query. When I moved to Qbillion, another startup, the environment shifted once again.

Qbillion hosted itself on Google AppEngine using the Python runtime. Similar to PHP in some respects, I picked up this one quickly. I eventually transitioned the entire site from Django to a custom framework I wrote, GAEMVC, (viewable on Github) due to slow speed and massive overhead.
